# Phase 10: Autonomy and Self-Extension

## Status
- **Current Status:** Pending
- **Completion:** 0%

## Overview
Phase 10 is the final phase where SELF moves toward autonomous self-extension. This involves the system's ability to proactively identify its own needs, propose new capabilities, and manage its own lifecycle with minimal user intervention.

## Objectives
- Implement self-extension capabilities.
- Develop the "Self-Reflection" loop.
- Implement proactive capability discovery.
- Implement autonomous lifecycle management (backups, upgrades).
- Establish the framework for multi-agent collaboration.
- Finalize the system's "Model Independence" and "Composability" targets.

## Deliverables
- [ ] Self-reflection loop implementation.
- [ ] Capability discovery and proposal system.
- [ ] Autonomous backup and health management.
- [ ] Multi-agent coordination framework.
- [ ] Final evaluation of the system's autonomy and autonomy limits.
- [ ] User guide for autonomous interaction.

## Dependencies
- All previous phases.

## Risks
- **Unintended Agency**: The system taking actions that the user did not explicitly authorize or expect.
- **Complexity**: Autonomous systems are harder to debug and audit.
- **Cost**: Proactive actions may incur unexpected costs (token usage, etc.).

## Success Criteria
- The system can identify a gap in its knowledge and propose a specific research task to fill it.
- The system can proactively notify the user of a significant change in their identity or project progress.
- The system can autonomously perform routine maintenance (e.g., cleaning old logs, rotating keys).
- The user feels in control of the system's autonomy at all times.
